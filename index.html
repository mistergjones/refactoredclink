Prison is tough

So you’re going to prison. I’ve been there and done that. Whether you’re in for 3 years or 30 years, being prepared and knowing what to expect, how to react, and what decisions to make are your greatest tools. Prison is no ride in the park. It’s not fun. It’s full of pitfalls, disappointments, threats and psychological challenges. It’s about making everyday decisions, hundreds of times a day, in the ultimate game of survival, but this is no game.

Post Release

When you make it through your time, your mind will be blown away by the choices you have once you get out, the labels people will put on you, including whether you will be accepted by friends or family. You will be at the mercy of your parole officer’s expectations about what you can and can’t do as you reintegrate back into society.

Being Prepared

Whether you’re in the Melbourne Remand Centre, A, B, or C category prisons, I can prepare you for what to expect, the decisions you will need to make, and how to act. I can’t prepare you for every scenario but I can make you aware through my experiences about what can and will happen along your journey through the system. You need to be smart; you need to be thinking ahead of what’s coming next; and you need to be ready to act. And when you’re ready to reintegrate into society, I will be there to walk you through “coming out.” My reality-based courses offer powerful insights and tools to prepare you for prison—and your re-entry into society when you’ve finished your time.
